# Energy Bands

Energy bands describe the allowed and forbidden energy states of electrons in a solid. They form the foundation of semiconductor physics and explain the electrical behavior of conductors, semiconductors, and insulators.

This page introduces the basic concepts of energy bands and illustrates how doping modifies the electronic properties of semiconductors.

---

## Overview

In isolated atoms, electrons occupy discrete energy levels.

When a large number of atoms form a crystal, these energy levels split into continuous energy bands due to atomic interactions.

The most important bands are:

- **Valence Band (VB)** – The highest occupied energy band at low temperature.
- **Conduction Band (CB)** – The energy band where electrons can move freely.
- **Band Gap (Eg)** – The forbidden energy region separating the valence and conduction bands.

## Intrinsic Semiconductor

An intrinsic semiconductor contains no intentional impurities.

At thermal equilibrium,

- The valence band is almost completely filled.
- The conduction band contains very few electrons.
- Electrical conductivity is relatively low.

## Extrinsic Semiconductor

The electrical properties of semiconductors can be modified by introducing dopant atoms.

### n-type Semiconductor

Donor atoms contribute additional electrons.

As a result,

- Electron concentration increases.
- The Fermi level shifts toward the conduction band.

---

### p-type Semiconductor

Acceptor atoms create holes in the valence band.

Consequently,

- Hole concentration increases.
- The Fermi level shifts toward the valence band.

## Fermi Level

The Fermi level represents the energy at which the probability of electron occupation is 50% under thermal equilibrium.

Its position indicates the electrical characteristics of a semiconductor.

- Near the conduction band → n-type semiconductor
- Near the valence band → p-type semiconductor
- Near the middle of the band gap → intrinsic semiconductor

## Importance in Semiconductor Devices

Energy band theory explains the operation of many semiconductor devices, including:

- PN junctions
- MOS capacitors
- MOSFETs
- Bipolar junction transistors (BJTs)
- CMOS integrated circuits

Understanding energy bands is essential for analyzing carrier transport, threshold voltage, and semiconductor device operation.
